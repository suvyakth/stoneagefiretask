Stone age fire













4 uses of fire task

1.One use of fire is for cooking meat to eat.
2.Second use of fire is heat to keep you warm.
3.Third use of fire is it gives you light to even see during night and dark caves.
4.They would also use the fire for hunting.

Four steps to make a fire.

1.First to make a fire (â€˜be sure to have a very dry surface and clear away leaves Etc away) you have to get materials: Tinder dried materials such as shredded bark, leaves or grass, small sticks or small branches, and Fuel Wood.

2.Take tinder material and make a ball in the place you want to make a fire ,take your small sticks and put them around like a tea following, lean pieces of Fuel wood against the small sticks.

3.Then take a long piece of Fuel wood and take a flint stone and rub it against the wood and hold it with your leg so it doesn't move.(rub near the ball that you had made previously)

4.Lastly the rubbing will start to become a spark. At this time you can blow on the sparkes, a little time later you will see a FIRE!!!

SAFETY:: You have to have sand or water if the fire starts to spread to put the fire off.

Skills they needed in order to survive

The skills they needed to survive are:
  • Hunting the only way they had discovered to survive. (food resource)
  • Making tools out of many types of stone, which they knew was the only way to hunt animals.
  • Having the sense to follow animal herds when the resources of the place they lived had ended.
